Every device on your local network independently pulling the same 2GB OS update from the internet is a bandwidth catastrophe. This prototype transforms idle storage across your local network into a distributed, encrypted P2P cache — so the second download is always local.

Your smart TV, office PCs, and workstations become a shared, zero-trust cache layer.

How It Works

  • STEP 01
    Chunk & EncryptWhen any device downloads a large file, it is split into encrypted 512KB chunks. Chunk metadata (hash, source, expiry) is broadcast to the local mesh.
  • STEP 02
    Idle Storage DistributionChunks are distributed across idle storage of participating devices. No single device stores the full file — it's spread and redundant by design.
  • STEP 03
    Local-First RetrievalWhen device #2 requests the same file, the mesh coordinator checks local peers first. Matching chunks are pulled via gigabit LAN — far faster than WAN.
  • STEP 04
    Fallback to CloudAny chunks not available locally are pulled from the origin server and simultaneously distributed to the mesh, improving future coverage.

Use Cases

Corporate Office IT
Rolling out OS patches or software updates to 500 machines? Pull once from the internet, distribute the rest over internal gigabit LAN — saving terabytes monthly.
School Networks
Schools with limited internet bandwidth can distribute educational software and curriculum packages across student devices via local Wi-Fi mesh.
Rural & Bandwidth-Limited Networks
Communities sharing a single satellite connection can dramatically reduce external traffic by caching popular content locally across households.
Smart Home Ecosystems
IoT firmware updates distributed across smart TVs, hubs, and PCs — no single device needs the full cloud download twice.
