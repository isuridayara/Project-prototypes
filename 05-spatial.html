What if your desk was a control surface? No touch screen. No expensive hardware. Just a webcam pointing down and computer vision tracking your hands.

Define zones anywhere on any physical surface and bind them to any system action. Tap the corner of your desk to skip a track. Slide your finger along an edge to change volume. Your desk becomes an invisible macro pad.

How It Works

  • STEP 01
    Surface CalibrationA downward-facing webcam establishes a reference map of the desk surface using corner markers or automatic homography. Takes under 10 seconds.
  • STEP 02
    Zone DefinitionUsers draw zones on a calibration UI (or by physically gesturing in front of the camera). Each zone is bound to a system macro: keypress, shell command, media key, or API call.
  • STEP 03
    Real-Time Hand TrackingA MediaPipe-based CV model tracks fingertip positions at 30fps. Gesture types (tap, hold, swipe, pinch) are classified and mapped to zone interactions.
  • STEP 04
    System Command DispatchRecognized gestures within mapped zones fire OS-level input events or API calls — indistinguishable from real keyboard or mouse input to target applications.

Use Cases

Accessibility Tools
Users with limited fine motor control can interact with a large physical surface using gross motor movements — no precision clicking required.
Streaming & Content Creation
Streamers can trigger OBS scene changes, mute/unmute, and run macros without looking away from the camera — the desk becomes an invisible Stream Deck.
Music Production
Producers can assign desk zones to DAW controls — play/stop, loop toggle, track arming — keeping hands free for instruments during recording.
Industrial Control Panels
Factory or lab environments can project control interfaces onto any horizontal surface — no touchscreen contamination risks, fully sanitizable surfaces.
